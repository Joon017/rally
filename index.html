03 — Workflow

Six steps.
One natural-language brief.

Rally is structured the way a good sourcing analyst works — clarify the brief, plan the search, execute, qualify, extract, and track coverage until the goal is met.

STEP 01

Clarify requirements

Rally reads your brief and asks targeted follow-ups: geographic scope, qualification thresholds, exclusion filters, contact requirements, output format, target quantity.

geo scopefilterscontact reqsoutput
STEP 02

Build a search plan

Before browsing, the agent assembles a structured strategy: which directories, map sources, niche listings, social platforms, and fallback paths to follow — and what qualification signals to watch for.

directoriesmapsniche listingssignals
STEP 03

Discover across the web

Rally searches Google, Maps, business directories, social platforms, booking platforms, and public databases — and dynamically expands into adjacent paths as new sources surface.

searchmapssocialpublic DBs
STEP 04

Qualify against criteria

Each candidate gets inspected: page features, technology signals, social activity, review thresholds, software fingerprints. Rally's filtering is reasoning-based, not keyword-based.

page inspectiontech signalsreview countsfiltering
STEP 05

Extract and verify contacts

Public emails, contact forms, phone numbers, Instagram, LinkedIn, WhatsApp — extracted, deduplicated, and verified where possible. Structured output you can pipe straight into outreach.

emailformsIG / LIWhatsApp
STEP 06

Track coverage to goal

Goal-based execution. Rally keeps running — retrying failed sources, expanding breadth, exploring alternative paths — until the target count is reached or the search space is exhausted.

retriesmemorycoveragecompletion

08 — FAQ

Answers, on the record.

The questions we hear most from operators evaluating Rally against their existing sourcing stack.

Q.01How is Rally different from Apollo or ZoomInfo?+
Apollo and ZoomInfo are static databases — they answer "which companies exist?" using pre-indexed datasets, which means they struggle with smaller, newer, niche, or local businesses, and they can't reason about nuanced criteria like "uses Square POS" or "no loyalty program." Rally researches the live web in real time and qualifies each business against your specific requirements before returning it.
Q.02How is Rally different from Clay?+
Clay is excellent at enrichment — turning a list you already have into a more useful list. Rally focuses on the earlier, harder problem: discovering and qualifying the businesses in the first place. The two tools compose well; Rally produces the seed list, Clay enriches it further.
Q.03Can I just use ChatGPT or Claude with web search?+
For one-off lookups, sure. But generic LLMs lack persistent sourcing state, retry logic, deduplication, coverage tracking, and goal-based execution — they tend to return five loosely-relevant matches and stop. Rally is purpose-built for sourcing as a workflow, not as a chat turn.
Q.04Where does the data come from? Is this legal?+
Rally only uses publicly available web data — the same pages a human researcher would consult: search engines, business directories, public maps listings, public social profiles, and business websites. Contacts are limited to publicly published information. Rally does not access private data, gated platforms, or paid databases.
Q.05How does goal-based sourcing work?+
You specify a target — say, 500 qualified leads — and Rally keeps running. It retries failed sources, expands into adjacent discovery paths, and tracks sourcing memory between runs so coverage compounds. The run completes when the target is met or the search space is genuinely exhausted.
Q.06What does pricing actually look like?+
Pricing on this page is illustrative ahead of launch. Rally will support a free tier, a flat operator subscription with pay-per-search usage, and custom volume pricing for agencies and teams. The goal is to make high-quality sourcing meaningfully cheaper than the traditional Apollo + Clay + intern stack.
